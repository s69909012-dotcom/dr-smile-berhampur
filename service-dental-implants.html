A missing tooth is not just a cosmetic problem. It affects how you eat, how you speak, and how you feel about yourself. Over time, the gap left by a missing tooth causes neighbouring teeth to shift and bone loss in the jaw. Dental implants are the only permanent solution that prevents all of this — and they look and feel exactly like natural teeth.

What is a Dental Implant?

A dental implant is a small titanium screw that is placed into the jawbone — acting as an artificial tooth root. Once it bonds with the bone, a crown is placed on top. The result is a tooth that looks, feels and functions exactly like your natural tooth. Nobody can tell the difference.

Implant vs Other Options

Removable Denture

Takes out at night. Can slip while eating. Does not prevent bone loss. Requires adhesive. Less comfortable over time.

🏆 Dental Implant

Permanent. Never removes. Prevents bone loss. No adhesive needed. Feels like natural tooth. Lasts a lifetime.

Dental Bridge

Requires grinding healthy teeth. Does not prevent bone loss. Needs replacement over time. Harder to clean.

The Implant Process — Step by Step

1

Consultation and X-Ray

We examine your teeth and jaw. An X-ray or CT scan confirms there is enough bone for the implant. We explain everything and answer all your questions before starting.

2

Implant Placement

The titanium implant is placed into the jawbone under local anaesthesia. The procedure is painless. Most patients are surprised at how comfortable it is.

3

Healing Period

The implant bonds with the jawbone over a few months. During this time a temporary tooth is placed so you never have a visible gap.

4

Crown Placement

Once healed, a custom-made permanent crown is attached. It matches your natural teeth perfectly. Your new tooth is complete.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is dental implant surgery painful?

No. The procedure is done under local anaesthesia so you feel no pain during the surgery. Mild soreness for a day or two after is normal and managed with prescribed medication.

Q: How long does a dental implant last?

With proper care — regular brushing, flossing and dental checkups — a dental implant can last a lifetime. The crown on top may need replacement after many years of wear.

Q: Am I a good candidate for dental implants?

Most healthy adults with missing teeth are good candidates. Adequate jawbone is needed to support the implant. Patients with diabetes or bone disease need evaluation first. A consultation will confirm if implants are right for you.

Q: How long does the implant process take?

The full process from implant placement to final crown takes a few months — mainly because the implant needs time to bond with the jawbone. The actual procedures are quick and comfortable.
