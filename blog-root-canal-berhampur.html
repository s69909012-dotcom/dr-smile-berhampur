What is Root Canal Treatment?

Inside every tooth, there is a soft tissue called the pulp. This pulp contains nerves and blood vessels. When the pulp gets infected — usually because of a deep cavity or a cracked tooth — it causes severe pain. Root canal treatment removes this infected pulp, cleans the inside of the tooth, and seals it. The tooth is saved. The pain is gone.

Think of it this way — root canal does not cause pain. It relieves the pain that was already there.

Root Canal Myths vs Real Facts

❌ Myth

"Root canal treatment is extremely painful"

✅ Fact

This is the most common fear among patients in Berhampur — and it is completely wrong. Root canal is done under local anaesthesia. You feel nothing during the procedure. The pain you fear is actually the pain of the infection — which the root canal removes. Most patients feel immediate relief after the treatment.

❌ Myth

"It is better to just remove the tooth instead of root canal"

✅ Fact

Removing a tooth might seem like the easier option — but it creates new problems. The gap left behind causes neighbouring teeth to shift, bone loss in the jaw, and chewing difficulties. Root canal saves your natural tooth — which is always the better option. A natural tooth is stronger and more comfortable than any replacement.

❌ Myth

"Root canal treatment takes many sittings and weeks to complete"

✅ Fact

With modern dental equipment, most root canal treatments in Berhampur can be completed in one or two sittings. Simple cases are often done in a single visit. The idea that RCT takes weeks comes from older methods that are no longer used.

❌ Myth

"A tooth that has had root canal will not last long"

✅ Fact

A properly done root canal followed by a crown can last a lifetime. Many people in Berhampur and across India have teeth that had root canal treatment decades ago and are still working perfectly fine. The key is proper treatment and regular dental checkups after.

❌ Myth

"If the pain goes away, I don't need root canal anymore"

✅ Fact

This is a dangerous myth. When tooth pain suddenly stops, it often means the nerve inside has died — not that the problem is gone. The infection is still there and can spread to the bone and surrounding teeth. Visiting a dentist in Berhampur as soon as possible is still necessary.

❌ Myth

"Root canal causes illness in other parts of the body"

✅ Fact

This myth has been completely disproved by modern dental science. Root canal treatment is safe. It removes infection from the tooth — it does not spread it. This old story comes from research done over a hundred years ago that has long been discredited.

When Do You Need Root Canal Treatment?

⚠️ See a Dentist in Berhampur If You Have:

  • Severe toothache that does not go away
  • Sensitivity to hot or cold that lingers for minutes
  • Darkening or discolouration of the tooth
  • Swelling or tenderness in the gums near the tooth
  • A pimple-like bump on the gum that keeps coming back
  • Pain when biting or chewing

What Happens During Root Canal Treatment?

1

X-ray and Diagnosis

The dentist takes an X-ray to see the shape of the root and check for infection in the surrounding bone.

2

Local Anaesthesia

The area is numbed completely. You will feel no pain during the procedure — just some pressure occasionally.

3

Cleaning the Pulp

The dentist removes the infected pulp from inside the tooth and cleans the canals thoroughly.

4

Sealing the Tooth

The cleaned canals are filled and sealed with a special material to prevent any future infection.

5

Crown Placement

A crown is placed on top of the treated tooth to protect it and restore full chewing function.

After Root Canal — What to Expect?

Most patients in Berhampur feel mild soreness for a day or two after root canal — this is completely normal. You can manage it with the medicines your dentist prescribes. Within a few days, you should feel completely fine and be able to eat normally.

Avoid chewing hard food on the treated tooth until the crown is placed. After the crown, you can use the tooth normally like any other tooth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is root canal treatment painful in Berhampur?

No. Root canal is done under local anaesthesia so you feel no pain during the procedure. The treatment actually relieves the pain caused by the infection inside the tooth.

Q: How many sittings does root canal take in Berhampur?

Most cases are completed in one to two sittings. Complex cases may need more. Your dentist will give you a clear idea after examining the tooth.

Q: Can I eat normally after root canal treatment?

Yes, but avoid hard or chewy food on the treated side until the crown is placed. After the crown, you can eat completely normally.

Q: Is root canal better than tooth removal?

Yes, in most cases. Saving your natural tooth is always better than removing it. Root canal preserves your tooth and avoids the problems caused by a gap in your mouth.

Q: How do I know if I need root canal or just a filling?

Only a dentist can tell for sure after examining the tooth and taking an X-ray. Generally, if the infection has reached the pulp, a filling is not enough — root canal is needed.
