Root canal treatment has an unfair reputation for being painful. The truth is — it is the infection causing the pain, not the treatment. Root canal actually removes that pain. At Dr. Smile Dental Clinic in Berhampur, we perform root canal treatment gently and comfortably under local anaesthesia.

What is Root Canal Treatment?

Inside every tooth is a soft tissue called the pulp — containing nerves and blood vessels. When a deep cavity or crack allows bacteria to reach the pulp, it gets infected and causes severe pain. Root canal treatment removes the infected pulp, cleans the inside of the tooth, and seals it. The tooth is saved. The pain is gone.

Common Myths — Cleared!

❌ Myth

"Root canal is extremely painful"

✅ Fact

Root canal is done under local anaesthesia — you feel no pain during the procedure. It actually relieves the severe pain caused by the infection.

❌ Myth

"Better to remove the tooth than get RCT"

✅ Fact

Saving your natural tooth is always better. Removing it causes neighbouring teeth to shift and bone loss in the jaw. Root canal preserves your natural tooth permanently.

❌ Myth

"RCT takes many sittings and weeks to complete"

✅ Fact

With modern equipment, most root canal treatments are completed in one or two sittings. The procedure is quick and comfortable.

Signs You May Need Root Canal Treatment

⚠️ Visit Us in Berhampur If You Have:

  • Severe toothache that does not go away
  • Sensitivity to hot or cold that lingers
  • Swelling or tenderness in the gums
  • Darkening or discolouration of the tooth
  • A pimple-like bump on the gum
  • Pain when biting or chewing

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is root canal treatment painful in Berhampur?

No. It is done under local anaesthesia and is completely painless. Most patients are surprised at how comfortable the procedure is.

Q: How many sittings does root canal take?

Most cases are completed in one to two sittings. Complex cases may need more. Your dentist will give you a clear timeline after examination.

Q: How long does a root canal treated tooth last?

With a proper crown and good oral hygiene, a root canal treated tooth can last a lifetime. Regular checkups help monitor its condition.

Q: Is root canal better than tooth removal?

Yes, in most cases. Saving your natural tooth is always the better option. Tooth removal leads to other problems like bone loss and shifting of neighbouring teeth.
