Why Do Teeth Get Yellow?

Teeth get stained for many reasons — tea and coffee are the most common culprits in Berhampur households. Tobacco, pan masala, certain foods, aging, and some medications also cause discolouration. Brushing alone cannot remove these deep stains — professional whitening is needed.

Is Teeth Whitening Safe?

Yes — professional teeth whitening done by a qualified dentist is completely safe. The whitening agents used are carefully controlled to protect your gums and enamel. Home whitening kits from shops are often not properly regulated and can cause sensitivity or damage if misused.

At Dr. Smile Dental Clinic in Berhampur, we always check your teeth and gums before starting any whitening treatment to make sure it is the right option for you.

Who is Teeth Whitening For?

Teeth whitening is suitable for most adults with healthy teeth and gums. It works best for stains caused by food, drinks, and tobacco. It does not work on crowns, veneers, or fillings — only natural teeth. Your dentist will advise if whitening is the right choice for your specific situation.

How Long Do Results Last?

With proper care — avoiding heavy tea, coffee, and tobacco — whitening results can last a long time. Touch-up treatments are available to maintain your brighter smile.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is teeth whitening painful?

Most patients experience no pain. Some may feel mild sensitivity during or after treatment — this is temporary and goes away within a day or two.

Q: How white will my teeth get?

Results vary depending on the original shade of your teeth and the type of staining. Your dentist will give you a realistic expectation before starting treatment.

Q: Can everyone get teeth whitening?

Most adults with healthy teeth can. Whitening is not recommended for children, pregnant women, or people with certain dental conditions. A checkup is needed first.

Q: How is professional whitening different from toothpaste?

Whitening toothpastes remove surface stains only. Professional whitening penetrates deeper into the tooth to remove stubborn stains — giving much more noticeable results.
