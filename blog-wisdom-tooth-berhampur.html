Wisdom Tooth Pain
in Berhampur

Should you remove it or keep it? Here is everything you need to know — in plain, simple language.

📅 May 23, 2026 ⏱️ 5 min read 🏥 Dr. Smile Dental Clinic, Berhampur
Wisdom tooth pain is one of the most common reasons people in Berhampur suddenly show up at a dental clinic — sometimes in the middle of the night. The pain can be sudden, sharp, and very uncomfortable. But should you always remove it? Not necessarily. Let me explain.

The wisdom tooth — also called the third molar — is the last tooth to grow. It usually appears between the ages of 17 and 25. Some people in Berhampur never have any problem with it. Others face swelling, infection, or severe pain. The difference depends on how and where the tooth grows.

Why Does Wisdom Tooth Cause Pain?

Your jaw already has all its teeth by the time the wisdom tooth tries to come in. So there is often not enough space. When a wisdom tooth cannot grow properly, it gets stuck under the gum or pushes against the neighbouring tooth. This is called an impacted wisdom tooth — and it is the main cause of pain.

In Berhampur, many young people between 18 and 25 come to us with this exact problem. The pain usually starts at the back of the mouth, and sometimes spreads to the jaw, ear, or even the head.

Remove or Keep? — Here is the Simple Answer

❌ Remove It When:

  • Tooth is impacted and causing pain
  • Repeated infections in the area
  • Pushing and damaging nearby teeth
  • Cyst forming around the tooth
  • Tooth is partially visible and trapping food
  • Swelling that keeps coming back

✅ Keep It When:

  • Tooth has grown fully and straight
  • No pain or swelling at all
  • Easy to clean with a toothbrush
  • Not damaging neighbouring teeth
  • X-ray shows no impaction

The honest truth is — not every wisdom tooth needs to be removed. If it is growing properly and not causing any problem, leave it alone. But if it is impacted or infected, removing it early is always better than waiting for it to get worse.

Common Myths About Wisdom Tooth — Cleared!

❌ MYTH

Wisdom tooth removal is extremely painful

This is the biggest fear among people in Berhampur. In reality, the procedure is done under local anaesthesia — you feel pressure, not pain. Most patients are surprised by how manageable it is. The discomfort after the procedure is normal and lasts only 2-3 days.

❌ MYTH

You must remove all 4 wisdom teeth at once

Not true. A dentist removes only the wisdom teeth that are causing problems. If one side is perfectly fine, there is no reason to touch it. Each tooth is evaluated separately.

❌ MYTH

Wisdom tooth pain will go away on its own

Sometimes the pain reduces temporarily — but if the tooth is impacted or infected, the problem does not go away. It comes back, usually worse than before. Ignoring it for months only makes the treatment more complicated later.

❌ MYTH

Young people should wait before removing wisdom teeth

Actually, the opposite is true. Younger patients recover faster from wisdom tooth removal. The bone is softer and the roots are not fully formed, making the procedure easier. Waiting too long can make it more complicated.

❌ MYTH

Home remedies can cure wisdom tooth pain permanently

Clove oil, salt water rinse, and pain tablets can reduce the pain temporarily. But they cannot fix an impacted tooth or an infection. These are short-term solutions only — not a permanent cure.

Warning Signs — Go to a Dentist Immediately!

⚠️ Don't Wait If You Have:

  • Swelling in the jaw or cheek that is spreading
  • Difficulty opening your mouth fully
  • Fever along with tooth pain
  • Bad taste or smell coming from the back of the mouth
  • Pain spreading to the ear or neck
  • Gum around the tooth is red and bleeding

What Happens After Removal — Recovery Tips

Most people in Berhampur recover fully within 3 to 7 days after wisdom tooth removal. Here is what helps:

  • 🧊 Apply ice pack on the cheek for the first 24 hours
  • 🍚 Eat soft foods — rice, dal, curd for 2-3 days
  • 🚫 Avoid spicy food, smoking, and hard biting
  • 💊 Take prescribed medicines on time
  • 🪥 Gently rinse with warm salt water after 24 hours
  • 📞 Call the dentist if swelling increases after day 3

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I know if my wisdom tooth needs to be removed in Berhampur?

The best way is to visit a dentist in Berhampur for an X-ray. Pain, swelling, repeated infection, or damage to neighbouring teeth are the main signs that removal is needed.

Q: Is wisdom tooth removal safe?

Yes, it is a very common and safe procedure when done by a qualified dentist. Millions of people get wisdom teeth removed every year without any serious complications.

Q: How long does wisdom tooth removal take in Berhampur?

A simple extraction takes about 15-30 minutes. A surgical extraction for an impacted tooth may take 30-60 minutes. You go home the same day.

Q: Can I eat normally after wisdom tooth removal?

For the first 2-3 days, stick to soft foods like khichdi, curd, soup, and mashed foods. After that, you can gradually return to normal eating as the pain reduces.

Q: What is the right age to remove wisdom teeth in Berhampur?

There is no fixed age. But most dentists recommend removing a problematic wisdom tooth between ages 18 and 30, when recovery is faster and the procedure is simpler.
